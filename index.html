How To Use
💙 You don't need to do this perfectly. Any time spent doing this together is time well spent.
Before You Start
1
Find a calm, quiet moment — no rush. Turn off the TV. Sit close to him so he can see your face.
2
Make sure the phone isn't on silent. The mute switch on the side of the iPhone must be off. You should see no orange stripe.
3
Tap any button once to "wake up" the sound. iPhones require one tap before audio works. Tap Check-In to get started.
Step 1 — Check-In
1
Tap Check-In from the home screen. This takes about 30 seconds.
2
Read each question out loud to them. "Energy okay today?" — watch for a squeeze or signal.
3
Tap the answer that matches their response. You're tapping based on what they show you.
4
Choose Short (3 min) or Normal (6 min). When in doubt, always pick Short. Short is plenty.
⚠️ If they show pain or discomfort, tap "Yes" and then "Skip Today." Rest is also progress.
Step 2 — Beat Reps
1
Choose who's tapping before you press Play. "Caregiver taps" = you tap the big button when you notice them responding. "They tap" = hand them the phone to tap it themselves.
2
Start with Steady Beat — the simplest one. Tap Play and let the clicks run. Tap along yourself so they can follow your lead.
3
Say "Tap with me" or "Squeeze when you hear it." Keep it casual and calm. No pressure. There's no wrong answer.
4
The pattern card itself is the tap target — tap anywhere on it on the beat. 🟢 Green = on the beat 🟡 Yellow = close 🔴 Red = off beat. Every attempt counts.
5
Try Echo Pattern next if they're doing well. It plays the pattern, pauses, then plays again. The pause is their moment to respond.
6
Use Repeat ×3 to replay a pattern three times in a row. Repetition is how the brain builds new pathways. Three reps is a great session.
7
Tap Stop at any time — no explanation needed. If they seem tired, uncomfortable, or distracted, stopping is always the right call.
🎵 The +/− buttons change the speed. 70 BPM is a calm walking pace — a good default. Slower is always okay.
Step 3 — Log the Win
1
After the session, tap Win Log from the home screen. This takes less than a minute.
2
Pick what counts as today's win — even "Showed up" is a real win. These aren't achievements to earn. They're reminders that every session matters.
3
Choose a mood and add an optional note. Notes like "They squeezed twice on the echo!" are beautiful to look back on later.
4
Tap Save Today's Win. It's stored on this phone. View it anytime in History.
Exercise Types Explained
A
Steady Beat — just a regular pulse. Great warmup. Tap along together. No pattern to memorize.
B
Echo Pattern — hear it, then echo it. Play → pause → play again. The pause is their moment. Any response counts.
C
A/B Match — which one matched? Two patterns play. You pick which matched the first. A fun listening game.
D
Fill the Gap — one beat is missing. Pattern plays with a silent beat. Can they feel where it should be?
Tips From the Care Team
✅ Consistent short sessions (even 5 min, 3× a week) are better than long sessions once a week.
✅ Your voice matters as much as the app. Talk to them through each exercise.
✅ It's okay if they don't respond. Being present in the rhythm is still working.
⚠️ This app is a caregiver support tool. Always follow guidance from their medical team. Stop if anything seems wrong.

Welcome to NeuroBeat Daily
This app helps you run short, calm rhythm sessions with your loved one — right from your phone.

It takes less than 10 minutes and you'll always know exactly what to do.
💙 You don't need any musical experience. You just need to show up.

1
Check-In Ask how they're feeling. Choose Short or Normal session.
2
Beat Reps Choose who's tapping, press Play, then tap the pattern card on the beat.
3
Win Log Record what happened. Every session counts.
